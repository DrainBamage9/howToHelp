### Limits and Continuity (Multivariable)
Its possible to extend the idea of a limit to functions with more than 1 variable, for example:
$$\lim_{(x,y)\to(1,2)}f(x,y)$$
means you approach (1,2) along every possible path in the plane.

A function is continous at a point if 
- the limit exists,
- the function is defined there,
- the limit equals the function value.

Continuity in multiple variables is trickier because paths can approach a point infinitely many ways.

#### Example:
$$\lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)}\frac{x^2y}{x^2+y^2}$$
Step 1: Try path y=0
$$\frac{x^2(0)}{x^2+0}=0$$
Step 2: Try path x=0
$$\frac{0y}{0+y^2}=0$$
Step 3: try y=x
$$\frac{x^2(x)}{x^2+x^2}=\frac{x^3}{2x^2}=\frac{x}{2}$$
As x $\to$ 0, this $\to$ 0. All paths converge to 0, therefor the limit is 0.
