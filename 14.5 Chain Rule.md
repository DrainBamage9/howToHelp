### Chain Rule

The chain rule lets you differentiate a composition of multivariable functions.

For Example if, $$z=f(x,y),\quad x=g(t),\quad y=h(t)$$
then
$$\frac{dz}{dt}=f_x\frac{dx}{dt}+f_y\frac{dy}{dt}$$

In general the chain rule applies to whenever variables depend on another. Including nested and implicly defined relationships.

#### Example

Given
$$z=x^2+y^2,\quad x=t^2,\quad y=3t$$
Find dz/dt

Step 1. Compute partial
$$f_x=2x,\quad f_y=2y$$
Step 2. Compute deriv of x,y
$$\frac{dx}{dt}=2t,\quad \frac{dy}{dt}=3$$
Step 3. Apply chain rule
$$\frac{dz}{dt}=f_x\frac{dx}{dt}+f_y\frac{dy}{dt}=(2x)(2t) + (2y)(3)$$
Sub in $x=t^2, y=3t$:
$$=2(t^2)(2t)+2(3t)(3)$$
$$=4t^3+18t$$
And that is our final answer.
