# 15.1 Double Integrals over Rectangles

A double integral adds up values of a function over an area instead of along a line.
$$\int\int_R f(x,y)dA$$
If the region $R$ is a rectangle:
$$a\le x\le b,\quad c\le y\le d$$
then the integral become:
$$\int^b_a \int^d_c f(x,y)\ dy\ dx$$
or
$$\int^d_c \int^a_b f(x,y)\ dx\ dy$$

This can be easily understood be thinking $Area=x*y$, simply brought from algebra into calculus. So long as ab correlates to dx, and cd correlates to dy.  

## Exam Hack

Choose easiest order first. If integration with respect to x/y removes a variable faster. Do that.

## Example

If $f(x,y) = x+2y$, find the area on the region:
$$0\le x\le 2,\quad 0\le y\le1$$

Step 1. Choose inside/outside and integrate inside.
$$\int^2_0\int^1_0 (x+2y)\ dy\ dx$$
$$\int^1_0(x+2y)dy$$
x is a constant
$$xy+y^2|_0^1=x+1$$
Step 2. Integrate outside
$$\int_0^2(x+1)\ dx$$
$$x^2+x|^2_0=4$$
Our answer is $A=4$
