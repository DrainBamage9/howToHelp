# 15.5 Surface Area
Given a surface area equation
$$z = f(x,y)$$
The surface area over a region is defined as:
$$\int\int_R\sqrt{1+f_x^2+f_y^2}\ dA$$
Where:
$$f_x=\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}$$
$$f_y=\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}$$
### Exam hack
- Compute $f_x$ and $f_y$ first.
- Then simplify inside the square root before integration.

## Example
Find the area on the surface $z=x^2+2y+2$ that lies above xy-plane vertices (0,0), (1,0), (1,1).
$$T=(x,y)\ |\ 0\le x\le1,\ 0\le y\le x$$
Find partial derivatives
$$f_x=2x$$
$$f_y=2$$
Substitue into equation:
$$\int\int_R\sqrt{1+f_x^2+f_y^2}\ dA$$
$$\int^1_0\int^x_0\sqrt{1+ 2x^2+2^2}\ dy\ dx$$
$$\int^1_0\int^x_0\sqrt{4x^2+5}\ dy\ dx$$
$$\int^1_0x\sqrt{4x^2+5}\ dx$$
$$u=4x^2+5$$
$$\frac{du}{dx}=8x$$
$$du=8x\ dx$$
$$x\ dx=\frac18du$$
$$\frac18\int^1_0\sqrt{u}\ du$$
$$\frac18\cdot\frac23u^{3/2}$$
$$=\frac{1}{12}(4x^2+5)^{3/2} |^1_0$$
$$=\frac1{12}(27-5\sqrt5)$$
