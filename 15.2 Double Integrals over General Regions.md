# 15.2 Double Integrals over General Regions

Sometimes regions aren't nice rectangles but instead depend on another variable. Like below:
$$0\le x\le1$$
$$0\le y\le x$$
Then our area integral becomes:
$$\int^1_0\int^x_0f(x,y)\ dy\ dx$$
This defined region would be a triangle.

## Region Types

Type 1
$$a\le x\le b$$
$$g_1(x)\le y\le g_2(x)$$
In this case you would integrate dy first (inside).

Type 2
$$c\le y\le d$$
$$g_1(y)\le x\le g_2(y)$$
You would integrate dx first.

## Example

Evaluate:
$$\int^x_0(x+y)\ dy\ dx$$

Step 1. Inner Integral

$$\int^x_0(x+y)\ dy$$
$$xy+\frac{y^2}{2}$$
Evaluate:
$$x(x)+\frac{x^2}{2}$$
$$=\frac{3x^2}{2}$$

Step 2. Outer Integral
$$\int_0^1\frac{3x^2}{2}\ dx$$
$$=\frac{3x^3}{6}$$
$$=\frac12$$
