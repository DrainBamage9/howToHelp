### Tangent Planes and Linear Approximations

In single-variable calculus, the tangent line approximates a curve at a given point.

In multivariable calc, this is instead the tangent plane.

If $z=f(x,y)$ then the tangent plane at point (a,b) is:
$$z=f(a,b) + f_x(a,b)(x-a) + f_y(a,b)(y-b)$$
This gives the linear approximation:
$$f(x,y)\approx L(x,y)$$
near (a,b) where L(x,y) is the plane given above.

#### Example

$$f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2$$
Find tangent plane (1,2)

Step 1. Compute partials
$$f_x = 2x, \quad f_y=2y$$
At(1,2):
$$f_x=2,\quad f_y=4$$
Step 2. Evaluate
$$f(1,2)=1+4=5$$
Step 3. Plug into plane formula
$$z=5+2(x-1)+4(y-2)$$
Simplify
$$z=2x+4y-5$$
