# 14.8 Lagrange Multipliers

Simplified, the premise of Lagrange Multipliers is to find the min/max values of a function f(x,y) given a constraining equation g(x,y). An example of this would be maximum volume of a rectangular prism assuming its surface area is constrained to be a constant: k.
$$v(x,y,z)=xyz$$
$$SA(x,y,z)=2(xy+zy+zx)=k$$
Here the volume equation is our f, while our surface area equation is g.

In essence the extrema on this constant curve, k, their gradients are parallel.

Condition:
$$\nabla f=\lambda \nabla g$$
Where $\lambda$ is our Lagrange multiplier.
Simply solve the system
$$f_x=\lambda g_x$$
$$f_y=\lambda g_y$$
$$g(x,y)=k$$

## Example
Find extrema of $$f(x,y)=xy$$
constrained by
$$x^2+y^2=1$$
Step 1. Gradients
$$\nabla f=⟨f_x,f_y⟩=⟨y,x⟩$$
$$\nabla g=⟨g_x,g_y⟩=⟨2x,2y⟩$$

Step 2. Set equal and solve
$$y=\lambda \cdot 2x$$
$$x=\lambda \cdot 2y$$
Solve for $\lambda$
$$\lambda=\frac{y}{2x}$$
$$\lambda=\frac{x}{2y}$$
$$\frac{y}{2x}=\frac{x}{2y}$$
$$y^2=x^2$$
Therefor:
$$y=\plusmn x$$
Sub into constraint eqn:
$$x^2+x^2=1$$
$$x=\plusmn \frac{1}{\sqrt2}$$
Step 3.Now plug and play, we have 4 possible solutions to the equation.

$$f(\frac{1}{\sqrt2},\frac{1}{\sqrt2})=\frac12$$
$$f(-\frac{1}{\sqrt2},\frac{1}{\sqrt2})=-\frac12$$
$$f(\frac{1}{\sqrt2},-\frac{1}{\sqrt2})=-\frac12$$
$$f(-\frac{1}{\sqrt2},-\frac{1}{\sqrt2})=\frac12$$

Step 4. Solution

Max = $\frac12$

Min = $-\frac12$
